\begin{problem}
  Let $Y$ be the quasicircle (or Warsaw circle), the closed subspace of $\R^2$ consisting of a portion of the graph of $y = \sin\left( 1/x \right)$, the segment $[-1,1]$ of the $y$ axis, and an arc connecting these two pieces. Collapsing the segment of $Y$ in the $y$-axis gives a quotient map $f\colon Y\rightarrow S^{1}$.

  Prove that $f$ does not lift to the covering space $\R\rightarrow S^1$, even though $\pi_1(Y) = 0$.
\end{problem}
\begin{solution}
  Suppose there were a lift $\widetilde{f}\colon Y\rightarrow \R$ such that if $p$ is the covering map $t\mapsto e^{2\pi i t}$, then $p\circ \widetilde{f} = f$. 
\begin{center}
  % https://tikzcd.yichuanshen.de/#N4Igdg9gJgpgziAXAbVABwnAlgFyxMJZABgBoBGAXVJADcBDAGwFcYkQBlAPXIAIAdfgGMCAc14BNAPSC4MHMGIBfQXgC28XsgC05CpRBLS6TLnyEUeqjQYs2iEBMPGQGbHgJEyxanSat2QTV6HAALACNw4AAlJUNfGChReCJQADMAJwg1JD0QHAgkACYaRnpwmEYABVMPCxAMrFFQnBAbf3sQQQB3LFg8RlhgNLijdKycxBL8wsQyPzt2NDaQMorq2vN2RubWsZBM7NyaAqR52wCHNPilIA
    \begin{tikzcd}
    \mathbb{R} \arrow[d, "p"']         &                                               \\
    {S^1 \cong Y/\set{0}\times [-1,1]} & Y \arrow[lu, "\widetilde{f}"'] \arrow[l, "f"]
    \end{tikzcd}
\end{center}
If we let $1$ be the value that $\set{0}\times \left[ -1,1 \right]$ is mapped to under $f$, then we observe that $\widetilde{f} = p^{-1}\circ f$ will map $Y\setminus \set{0}\times \left[ -1,1 \right]$ homeomorphically to $\left( n,n+1 \right)$ for some $n\in \Z$; in particular, since $\set{0}\times \left[ -1,1 \right]$ is connected, it must be the case that $p^{-1}$ gives an injective map from $S^{1}\cong f(Y)$ to $\R$ --- specifically, a bijective map from $S^{1}$ to $\left( n,n+1 \right]$ or $\left[ n,n+1 \right)$. Yet, applying the intermediate value theorem by approaching $1\in S^{1}$ from either direction yields a contradiction, meaning such a lift cannot exist.
\end{solution}
