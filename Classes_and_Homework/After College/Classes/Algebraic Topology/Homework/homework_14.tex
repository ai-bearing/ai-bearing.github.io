\begin{problem}[Problem 1]
  Find simply connected covering spaces of $ \mathbb{RP}^2 $ and $S^2\vee S^1$.
\end{problem}
\begin{solution}
  We observe that $ \mathbb{RP}^2 = S^2/\left( x\sim -x \right) $ can be identified as a quotient space. Therefore, if we let $q\colon S^2\rightarrow \mathbb{RP}^2$ be the quotient map, then $q$ is a covering map as any open subset of $ \mathbb{RP}^2 $, upon taking preimages, maps to two antipodal homeomorphic open subsets of $S^2$. Thus, this is our desired (simply connected) covering space.

  In the case of $S^2\vee S^1$, we observe that from Homework 12, a covering space is given by $\R\sqcup S^2$ attached at $\left( 1,0,0 \right)\sim 0$. Since their intersection is a single point, and both $\pi_1\left( S^2 \right)$ and $\pi_1\left( \R \right)$ are the trivial group, it follows from a van Kampen argument that $\pi_1\left( \R\vee S^2 \right)$ is the trivial group, so this is a simply connected cover for $S^2\vee S^1$.
\end{solution}
