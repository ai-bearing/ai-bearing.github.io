\begin{problem}[Problem 1]
  Prove that if $X$ is a path-connected and locally-path connected space with $\pi_1(X)$ a finite group, then every map $X\rightarrow S^{1}$ is null-homotopic.
\end{problem}
\begin{solution}
  We know that if $f\colon X\rightarrow S^{1}$ is any map, then $f$ induces a homomorphism $f_{\ast}\colon \pi_1(X)\rightarrow \pi_1\left( S^1 \right) \cong \Z$. In particular, since $\pi_1(X)$ is finite, and $\Z$ is infinite with the only element of finite order being $0$, it follows that $f_{\ast}$ is the zero map.

  Thus, $f$ admits a lift to $\R$, which we call $ \widetilde{f} $ as $X$ is both path-connected and locally path-connected. Since $X$ is path-connected, so too is $ \widetilde{f}(X) \subseteq \R$, but this means that $ \widetilde{f}(X) $ is an interval, hence contractible. Thus, $ \widetilde{f} $ is null-homotopic, so upon applying the covering map, we get that $f$ is null-homotopic.
\end{solution}
