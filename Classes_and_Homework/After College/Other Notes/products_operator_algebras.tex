\subsection{Crossed Products of $C^{\ast}$-Algebras}%
If we have a group $\Gamma$ acting on a $C^{\ast}$-algebra $A$, then a question becomes how we may construct a $C^{\ast}$-algebra that encodes this action.

If a group is acting on another group, there is a construction known as the (outer) semidirect product that enables this. We will construct a $C^{\ast}$-algebra emerging from an action $\alpha\colon \Gamma\rightarrow \aut(A)$, which will be dubbed $A\rtimes_{\alpha}\Gamma$.
