\section{Young Diagrams}%
We recall from abstract algebra that in the symmetric group $S_n$, the conjugacy classes are given by the cycle types (hence by partitions of $n$), meaning that partitions of $n$ are in one to one correspondence with irreducible representations. Yet, is there more we can say? That is, is there a canonical assignment of irreducible representations to partitions that we can construct in a systematic manner?
\begin{definition}
  Let $\lambda = \left( \lambda_1,\dots,\lambda_n \right)$ be a partition of $n$. A \textit{Young diagram} associated to $\lambda$, denoted $Y_{\lambda}$.
\end{definition}
